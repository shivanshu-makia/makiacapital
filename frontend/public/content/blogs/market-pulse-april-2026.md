## Executive Summary

April 2026 marked a sharp reversal from March's broad sell-off. Indian equities rallied across the cap curve — Nifty 50 (+7.4%), Midcap (+13.5%), Smallcap (+18.4%) and SME (+24.0%) — supported by robust domestic liquidity and a renewed risk-on bid. IPO activity was mixed on the mainboard but resilient in SMEs, signalling selective rather than indiscriminate participation. While the rebound is encouraging, valuations are once again stretched against an unresolved oil shock and a still-fragile global backdrop. **Our view:** this is a liquidity-led rally, not yet an earnings-led one — selectivity will matter more than direction over the next two quarters.

## Key Market Developments

### India Pays to Play — INR 30 Bn Swap Keeps Maldives Out of China's Lap

India approved INR 30 Bn for the Maldives under the SAARC Currency Swap Framework this month, coinciding with Maldives repaying its earlier USD 400 Mn facility on the same day. Since 2012, RBI has extended cumulative USD 1.1 Bn in swap support to the island nation. The move bolsters INR's regional reserve currency credentials, supports India's sovereign bond sentiment and signals growing strategic depth in the Indian Ocean — a region increasingly contested by China. INR 30 Bn is a fraction of India's reserves but arguably its highest-return strategic deployment of the month.

### India Unlocks the Pacific — Landmark FTA with New Zealand

India and New Zealand have sealed a comprehensive Free Trade Agreement, kicking in by December 2026, with 100% duty-free market access for India and a USD 20 Bn FDI commitment over 15 years. Textiles, engineering and auto components are clear winners; dairy and sugar were carefully kept off the table. Bilateral services trade today is just USD 650–700 Mn against India's USD 400+ Bn in total services exports — a wide-open runway. The deal also secures 5,000 skilled-worker slots and 1,000 work-and-holiday visas annually for Indians.

### PSU Banks Caught Off Guard as RBI Refuses to Blink on ECL Deadline

RBI confirmed **1 April 2027** as the hard ECL implementation deadline, rejecting banks' request for an extension. Nifty PSU Bank fell 1.91% on the announcement — Union Bank –3.45%, Canara Bank –2.97%, SBI –1.48%, Bank of Baroda –1.71%. ECL forces upfront provisioning for future losses, squeezing profitability and capital. Private banks already sit comfortably with 2–4% provision buffers; **PSU lenders now face a 12-month sprint to build capital and overhaul risk models.** Expect this to be a major overhang on PSU bank multiples through FY27.

## Global & Geopolitical Developments — April 2026

### 01 — World Bank Warns: Commodity Prices to Hit a 4-Year High in 2026
The World Bank forecasts overall commodity prices to surge 16% in 2026, with energy prices up 24% and fertilizer costs up 31% — all driven by the Iran war disruption to the Strait of Hormuz, which carries 35% of seaborne crude trade. Brent is projected to average USD 86/bbl (up from USD 69 in 2025), with a worst case of USD 115. Developing economies face inflation of 5.1% and trimmed growth of 3.6%; precious metals are projected to rise 42% on safe-haven demand.

**India Impact:** Sustained input cost pressure on energy importers, agri-inputs and metals-consuming manufacturers. Commodity-linked Indian equities and EM allocations face structural headwinds through 2026.

### 02 — Oil Surges Past USD 125/bbl as US–Iran Deadlock Deepens
Brent crude for June delivery surged 6.2% to USD 125.36/bbl, with July delivery up 3.1% to USD 113.85. Benchmark US crude climbed 2.3% to USD 109.38/bbl — a dramatic move from the pre-war USD 70/bbl level in late February. Now in its ninth week, the Iran war shows no clear resolution path. Asian markets buckled instantly: Nikkei –1.6%, Hang Seng –1.3%, Sensex –1.2%.

**India Impact:** Sustained CAD pressure, OMC margin compression and INR weakness. Aviation, paints and chemicals face direct input cost shock.

### 03 — Emerging Markets Post Best Month in 4 Years on AI Surge
The MSCI Emerging Markets Index jumped 14.5% in April — its best monthly gain since 2022 and possibly the strongest in 17 years — driven by AI optimism in Asian tech as global AI capex crosses USD 300 Bn annually with projections of USD 600 Bn by 2026. Yet Brent at USD 125.36/bbl (~79% above pre-war levels) keeps oil-importing economies pressured.

**India Impact:** India risks under-participating in the AI-led EM rotation given its low semiconductor-equity weighting. Capital is rotating into Korea and Taiwan-led tech beta.

### 04 — Powell's Final Act: Wall Street Watches Every Word
S&P 500 +13% and Nasdaq +19% since 30 March, but earnings week is the test. Magnificent Seven earnings projected to grow 25% in 2026 — more than 2x the 11% for the rest of S&P 500. The Fed held rates at 3.50–3.75%, marking Powell's likely final meeting before his 15 May exit, with Kevin Warsh on track to replace him. CPI at 3.3% is the highest since May 2024; markets price only a 26% chance of any rate cut in 2026.

**India Impact:** Higher-for-longer US rates extend FII outflow risk and cap INR upside. A potential Warsh-led Fed pivot is a key 2H'26 watch.

### 05 — Foxconn Surges on AI Demand: A Bellwether for the Hardware Boom
Foxconn posted +30% YoY revenue in Q1, with growth concentrated in AI data centre racks and cloud & networking products. The new iPhone launch contributed to "significant" growth in consumer electronics. AI infrastructure demand is now a measurable revenue driver, not just a narrative.

**India Impact:** Constructive read-through for Indian EMS players (Dixon, Kaynes, Syrma) on the AI hardware super-cycle. India still needs to climb the value chain from assembly to advanced packaging to capture the bigger pool.

### 06 — UAE Exits OPEC After 55 Years
UAE — OPEC's third-largest producer contributing USD 77 Bn of its USD 455 Bn annual oil sales — exits effective 1 May after 55 years. The Iran war has already collapsed OPEC output 27% to 20.79 Mn bpd in March, the worst supply shock in decades. Free from quotas capping output at 3.2 Mn bpd, UAE could nearly double production once Hormuz reopens. With Kazakhstan tipped as next to leave, OPEC's grip on global oil pricing is genuinely at risk.

**India Impact:** **Structurally positive medium-term.** A weakened OPEC means more competitive pricing for Indian crude buyers — but only after the Hormuz disruption resolves. Near-term volatility remains the dominant feature.
